Democracy in the Crosshairs: How the Rajapaksa Era Redefined Modern Authoritarianism

A landmark report by Freedom House, titled Breaking Down Democracy, has highlighted the tenure of former Sri Lankan President Mahinda Rajapaksa as a case study in the rise of "illiberal democracy." The report examines how leaders in the 21st century—including Rajapaksa, Vladimir Putin, and Viktor Orbán—have moved away from traditional military coups in favor of "capturing" democratic institutions from within. ​According to the analysis, the Rajapaksa administration utilized a specific toolkit to consolidate power: ​Media Domination: Leveraging state-controlled media to marginalize political opponents. ​Institutional Capture: Weakening the independence of the judiciary and civil society. ​Nationalist Rhetoric: Using post-war triumphalism to justify the suppression of dissent. ​The report notes that while Rajapaksa was eventually unseated in the 2015 elections, his methods provided a blueprint for other "modern authoritarians" to validate their rule through the ballot box while simultaneously hollowing out democratic safeguards. ​Fact Check on Viral Claims Regarding recent social media speculation involving the so-called "Epstein files," investigative audits of the 2025-2026 document releases confirm that no Sri Lankan political figures, including Mahinda Rajapaksa, appear in the official records.
